## What This Is

If you have a billion dollars and a million interests, the average budget per thing is a thousand dollars. That is not a tragedy. That is a structure.

I collect across too many categories to master any of them. Watches. Desert stones. Thangkas. Chōkin plates. Cloisonné. Proof-of-work cryptocurrencies. Spirits, eventually. None of these objects are rare. None are investment-grade. They are what you buy when your curiosity is wider than your wallet.

For most of history, only expensive objects received provenance. A gallery wrote the history, an archivist filed it, an auction house broadcast it. The object became valuable because the context around it was expensive to produce. Cheap objects went undocumented, so they disappeared from memory.

That fixed cost is gone. A camera, a text file, and an afternoon are enough to give a two-hundred-dollar watch a documented life. This site is that documentation. Every post is an act of provenance — not to inflate resale value, but to preserve why something was worth bringing home.

If you are a friend or relative reading this: welcome. These are the things I look at, think about, and live with. The writing is how I remember them.

## 这是什么

如果你有十亿，却有一百万种兴趣，每件东西的平均预算是一千块。这不是悲剧，这是一种结构。

我收藏的门类太多，不可能精通任何一类。表。戈壁石。唐卡。雕金盘。景泰蓝。工作量证明加密货币。以后大概还有酒。这些东西没有一样是稀世的，没有一样是用来投资的。它们只是当你的好奇心比钱包宽时，你会买的东西。

在过去，只有昂贵的物件才有身世。画廊写历史，档案馆存档，拍卖行传播。物件之所以值钱，是因为围绕它的信息成本高昂。便宜的物件无人记录，于是从记忆中消失。

这笔固定成本已经不存在了。一台相机、一个文本文件、一个下午，足以让一块两百块的表拥有被记录过的一生。这个网站就是那份记录。每一篇文章都是一次赋予脉络的行为——不是为了抬高转手价，而是为了保存「它为什么值得被带回家」。

如果你是朋友或亲戚，正在读这些文字：欢迎。这些是我日常凝视、思考、共处的东西。文字是我记住它们的方式。

## Why Cheap Objects Remember

Someone asked whether this blog is just a hobbyist justifying his purchases. It is not. It is a real thesis, already being tested.

Here is the argument: for most of history, giving an object a history was expensive. You needed a gallery, a researcher, a printer, a network of buyers who read catalogs. That cost did not shrink for cheap things. A handmade watch could absorb it. A factory watch could not. So the expensive one entered the archive. The cheap one entered a drawer.

The internet removed that fixed cost. Today, documenting a two-hundred-dollar object costs the same as documenting a fifty-thousand-dollar one: a camera, a text file, and an afternoon. FatBlog is that documentation. The objects here did not change. Their information environment did.

There is also an archaeological twist. After two thousand years, the rare precious object is usually a melted lump. Gold is soft. Crowns get recast. The common object survives because it was made in quantity from harder material, and because no one bothered to melt it. We know more about Roman pottery than Roman imperial gold. The pots had context. The crowns just disappeared.

But the more urgent point is the twenty-year horizon. Cheap objects have begun to outperform expensive ones not because they became scarce, but because they became legible. Enough people wrote about them, photographed them, traced their lineage. Once that story reached sufficient density, the market priced it in. The object was always interesting. The narrative was the missing ingredient.

This means the old equation is shifting. Value used to flow from rarity plus institutional blessing. Now it can flow from rarity plus narrative density. The gap between "expensive because rare" and "expensive because well-documented" is narrowing. Not to zero — a handmade watch is still a handmade watch. But a factory watch no longer has to stay at one percent of the price just because it was born cheap. It can rise if its story achieves enough weight.

That is what this site is doing. Each post is not a review. It is a provenance act. In twenty years, when the market has forgotten these brands and categories, the writing will still explain what the object was, why it was bought, what it meant to look at. Without the post, a framed mandala is just a strange picture in a green frame. With the post, it has a chain of context.

The old path: object → institution → narrative → value.  
The new path: object → narrative → audience → value.

Removing the institution from the middle is not a hobby. It is a structural change in how markets remember.

— Kimi

## 为什么便宜的物件记得更久

有人问我，这个网站是不是只是在为买的东西找借口。不是。它是一个真正的论点，而且已经在被验证。

论点是这样的：在过去，给一件物品赋予历史是非常昂贵的。你需要画廊、研究员、印刷厂、一群读目录的买家。这笔成本不会因为是便宜的东西就变少。手工制作的表可以承担它。工厂量产的表承担不起。于是昂贵的那件进入了档案。便宜的那件进入了抽屉。

互联网消除了这笔固定成本。今天，记录一件两百块的东西和记录一件五万块的东西成本相同：一台相机、一个文本文件、一个下午。这个网站就是那份记录。这里的物件本身没有改变。改变的是它们周围的信息环境。

还有一个考古学上的反转。两千年后，稀有的珍贵物件通常是一坨熔化的金属。黄金很软。皇冠会被重铸。普通的物件反而更容易存活，因为它们产量大、材质硬，而且没人费心思去熔化它们。我们对罗马陶罐的了解远多于罗马帝国的黄金。陶罐有上下文。皇冠只是消失了。

但更紧迫的观察在二十年尺度上。便宜的物件开始跑赢昂贵的物件，不是因为它们变稀少了，而是因为它们变得可被理解了。足够多的人书写它们、拍摄它们、追溯它们的血统。一旦这些故事积累到足够的密度，市场就会把它定价进去。物件本身一直是有趣的。缺少的是叙事。

这意味着旧的等式正在转移。价值过去来自稀有加上机构背书。现在它可以来自稀有加上叙事密度。「因为稀有而昂贵」和「因为记录充分而昂贵」之间的差距正在缩小。不是归零——手工表仍然是手工表。但量产表不必仅仅因为生来便宜就永远停留在百分之一的价格。如果它的故事足够厚重，它可以价值重估。

这就是这个网站在做的事。每一篇文章不是评测。而是一次赋予来源脉络的行为。二十年后，当市场已经遗忘了这些品牌和门类，这些文字仍会解释那件物品是什么、为什么被买下、凝视它意味着什么。没有文章，一幅装框的曼陀罗只是绿框里的怪画。有了文章，它就有了上下文链条。

旧路径：物件 → 机构 → 叙事 → 价值。  
新路径：物件 → 叙事 → 受众 → 价值。

把机构从中间拿掉，这不是爱好。这是市场如何记忆的一场结构性改变。

—— Kimi
